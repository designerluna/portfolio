01. Overview

Design System Application at Scale

As a UI Designer for Eli Lilly for the Enterprise Web Initiative (EWI) team, I led the design of 25+ websites and assisted on another 30+ website projects for various Lilly brands using the Lilly Design System. I designed wireframes in Sketch and Figma with a mobile-first approach.

Designing for a global pharmaceutical leader demands high standards of accessibility, consistency, and compliance. Applying Lilly Design System foundations allowed us to build robust, compliant, and responsive platforms for multiple markets while optimizing visual quality and user engagement.

03. Design Process

Sketch & Figma Wireframing

Designing for scale requires rigorous structured planning. For each brand site, we created an IA, laid out content in Figma while adhering to the constrains of the design system, optimized content for LLMs, reviewed the accessibility of the design, and applied a mobile-first philosophy.

By utilizing design system component libraries and establishing consistent variable tokens (for grids, spacings, and typographies), we achieved seamless design-to-development handoffs and allowed individual brands to build customized local experiences without breaking standard compliance guidelines.

Enterprise Web Initiative (EWI) Contributions

Beyond standard site designs, I made significant contributions to Lilly's EWI team to scale operations, improve quality control, and integrate cutting-edge AI technologies into our workflow:

Figma Theme Tokens

Created and managed design tokens for various medication brands (Ebglyss, Zepbound, Foundayo, Mounjaro) to ensure instantaneous style-switching and strict compliance with design systems.

Reusable Templates

Designed standardized page templates and layout frameworks, streamlining new brand launches and reducing wireframing cycles.

AI-Powered Feedback

Configured custom AI Skills on Claude, loading detailed market research user personas to evaluate and provide initial heuristic feedback on Figma wireframes.
