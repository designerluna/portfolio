WorldTrips Customer Portal Redesign

Rethinking and redesigning the self-service web experience for Client Zone and Student Zone members.

Client WorldTrips
My Role UX Researcher & Designer
Duration 6 Months
Platform Responsive Web (Mobile & Desktop)

01. Overview

Redesigning for Self-Service

WorldTrips is a travel insurance company offering various plans that include travel insurance benefits at different price points and benefit levels. The Client Zone portal serves travel medical insurance policyholders, and the Student Zone portal is designed for international student health insurance policyholders.

A complete redesign of the portal was necessary because the legacy platform was limited, failed to provide sufficient information, and drove high customer support call volumes. There was substantial agent and customer demand for an intuitive, comprehensive self-service portal.

My team consisted of myself as the lead UX researcher and designer of the portal, an analyst who documented the UI requirements, and a developer who offered feedback from a technical feasibility perspective.

The Goal

Complete phase 1 of the customer portal front-end development by designing and prototype-testing intuitive desktop and mobile versions of a new customer portal.

Key Objectives

  • Make the new customer portal intuitive and efficient.
  • Ensure the mobile experience is equally as capable and delightful as the desktop version.
  • Identify customer frustration points in the old portal and systematically address them.
  • Reduce the volume of customer service calls by placing critical resources in an accessible dashboard.

Survey Analysis & Customer Sentiment

The first phase of user research was aimed at understanding how customers experienced our legacy portal. I designed and deployed a survey prompting visitors to rate their experience during their session on Client Zone or Student Zone.

Purpose of Visit

The survey results showed that the primary reasons why customers are visiting Client Zone or Student Zone is to either file a claim, view their claim status, extend or renew their policy, and view their description of benefits.

Visitor Frustrations

Client Zone

Delayed claim listings, frequent technical issues, and a poor overall navigation experience.

Student Zone

Difficult navigation paths and confusion regarding how to locate or contact customer support.

Visitor Suggestions

Client Zone

Streamlining the claim filing process, modernizing the UI, and adding email updates for claim changes.

Student Zone

System infrastructure updates, alerts for pending items, and the ability to edit their profile settings.

Internal Interviews: Support Team Insights

To supplement customer surveys, I interviewed front-line customer service representatives to discover the key reasons why users call in. The support representatives highlighted the following major points:

  • ✕ Users call frequently for basic tasks unavailable on the portal, such as password resets and cancelling policies.
  • ✕ Users are confused by vague verbiage inside DocuSign documents.
  • ✕ Members do not realize they are required to fill out a HIPAA form to process claims.
  • ✕ The current portals are highly inconsistent across screen sizes and mobile browsers.

05. Usability Study

Testing & Finding Friction

After creating an initial prototype on Adobe XD, I facilitated 6 user testing sessions with participants of varying demographics. Half of the users tested the mobile version and the other half tested the desktop version.

Core Usability Study Insights

  • Some users had a small moment of confusion when they reached the login page, expecting to enter their email to register immediately.
  • Users wanted more visual separation between links in the main navigation and requested a live chat option.
  • Most users struggled to figure out how to switch between different travelers whose policy they had purchased.
  • One user requested to see email alerts confirming refund amounts upon policy cancellation.
  • The term "Description of Coverage" was found to be slightly confusing; "Description of Benefits" made more sense.
  • Users requested explicit maximum file size and file type details in the claim file upload form.

Usability Participant Feedback

  • • "[The label] 'Claim Number' is confusing."

06. Prototype Updates

Addressing the Friction Points

I addressed the issues mentioned by the usability study participants through target iterations on the Adobe XD prototype.

1. Context-Rich Claims Timeline

In the image below, the claim timeline has been changed to reflect the issue mentioned by Participant 1, who found the claim number label to be confusing. Though we have to have that label to differentiate claims, I also added other ways of organizing and labeling the claims by adding the field "date incurred", "status", and "provider". This also creates an opportunity for users to filter results.

Claim timeline accordion showing detailed context (V2)

2. Seamless Registration Flow

Since users initially thought they had to input their email to start their registration, I decided to change the flow of the registration process just in case users missed the "First time user?" link, as shown in the image below. By entering an email that has not yet been registered, a user will be prompted with a registration form with the email they entered preset to the registration email field. This is a more seamless experience for the user instead of showing an error message saying that the email is not associated with an account.

Revised landing page with intelligent login-to-registration transition (V2)

3. Multi-Policy Member Switcher

There were two issues that were able to be addressed with the addition of one feature. The first issue was that some users seemed to look for the "Add Policy" button in multiple places throughout the customer portal. Most users seemed look for the button at the top of the webpages. The second issue was that users seemed to be overwhelmed by the thought of seeing multiple policies while navigating the site, rather than seeing everything organized as a unique policy number or policyholder.

My colleague and I brainstormed and decided to create a dropdown at the top of every page where a user can view a single policy at a time, switch between the policies they are viewing, and have the option to add a new policy. The images below show how a new policy is added. The "viewing" dropdown will also be found at the top of the page so users can easily switch between policyholders at anytime.

1. Dropdown showing single policyholder
2. Form fields to append another policy
3. Updated switcher showing both policies

4. Verbiage Update: Description of Benefits

Another issue that needed to be address was the the button verbiage that said "Description of Coverage". It wasn't too confusing for users to understand, but some users recommended to change it to "Description of Benefits" for extra clarity, which is reflected in the image below.
