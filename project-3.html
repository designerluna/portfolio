UX & Accessibility

Grange & Integrity Insurance

Improving web accessibility and component library refactoring for WCAG 2.1 compliance.

Client Grange Insurance
My Role UX Designer
Duration 2 Months
Platform Responsive Web

01. Overview

The Stage for Access

The team at Grange Insurance contracted our team to help improve the accessibility on their two websites, www.grangeinsurance.com and www.integrityinsurance.com. Grange Insurance is a regional insurance company that offers a variety of insurance products, including auto, home, life, and business insurance.

The team consisted of a Project Manager, Web Developer, UX Strategist, and myself as the UX Designer.

Objectives

  • Audit Grange Insurance and Integrity Insurance sites to check for WCAG 2.1 compliance.
  • Use findings to improve the accessibility in their current component system library.
  • Create training material and governance plan to establish long-term accessibility goals for the Grange Insurance Product & Engineering team.

02. Strategy

Team Roles & Responsibilities

Improving digital accessibility is a multi-disciplinary effort. Our team worked in parallel to audit the interfaces, update component patterns, and set up governance structures.

UX Designer (Myself)

  • Ran an accessibility audit for both Grange and Integrity Insurance websites on the page and template level.
  • Documented audit findings and presented summary of findings to the Grange Insurance team.
  • Created training material for the Grange Insurance team and split accessibility responsibilities to help make accessibility a team effort.
  • Assisted in creating the governance plan read-out.

Web Developer

  • Added critical accessibility documentation within the existing component library.
  • Used accessibility audit findings to update components so they all complied with WCAG 2.1 requirements.

UX Strategist

  • Interviewed Grange Insurance Product & Engineering team members to find opportunities to include accessibility earlier in the design and development process.
  • Assisted in creating the governance plan read-out.

Project Manager

  • Organized requirements for accessibility audit and component library refresh.
  • Assisted in creating the governance plan read-out.

03. Deliverables

Accessibility Audit Report

The main deliverable that I worked on was the accessibility audit documentation, which can be reviewed by clicking the button below. The training library I created cannot be shared as it is on Grange Insurance's private internal site.

✔ WCAG 2.1 Audit Summary

1. Keyboard Accessibility (Success Criterion 2.1.1)

Verified that all interactive components (buttons, links, form elements, select dropdowns) are fully operable via keyboard navigation alone, establishing a logical tab order.

2. Color Contrast Ratios (Success Criterion 1.4.3)

Evaluated contrast levels of text, background, and UI boundaries. Adjusted component colors to meet the minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text.

3. Name, Role, Value (Success Criterion 4.1.2)

Refactored form fields, labels, and aria-attributes to ensure assistive technologies (like screen readers) correctly read component states, tags, and inputs.
